This is a dancing game

It’s like a fun 1 min silent dance tribute to David Bowie. Suitable for 4 - 20 players. It’s a game of elimination and you will play several rounds.
It's simpler to play than the rules suggest.

It’s quite possible that there will be no winners, but you could be heroes.

What you need

A phone you can get online with.
A pair of headphones.

How to play.

Assemble your players in a circle.
Go to the Bowie Hip sync page.
Someone needs to count down to the game start. 3..2..1..GO!
On 'Go', everyone presses 'Dance' at the same time.
The game then plays 1 of 4 Bowie tracks at random.
Everyone needs to silently dance to the track they hear.
Look around and figure out who is dancing to the same music as you.
Then move across the dance floor so you are dancing with them in a group.
All of the tracks are 1 min long.
When the music stops, the name of the track you've been dancing to will be revealed on screen.

Win / Lose

If everyone in your group is dancing to the same tune, YAY!
If not then your whole group is out!.
Keep going with new rounds until you have 2 players left. They are the winners.

Notes

If you think someone in your group is dancing to a different tune, you will need to find a way to expel them before the round is up or you will all be out.

If there are multiple groups all dancing to the same tune, the biggest one stays in, all the others go out...

You are not allowed to sing along or mouth along to the songs.

Try to dance as expressively as you can.

After a couple of rounds you are likely develop a shorthand. If you like you can start to reduce the length of the rounds to 30 or even 15 seconds.
